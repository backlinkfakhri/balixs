Can Foreigners Purchase Real Estate in Bali? Understanding Property Ownership and Rights

In Bali’s real estate world, becoming a property owner is a long and complicated process involving legal and cultural factors. As a stranger wanting to invest in this beautiful island, you must know the different ownership and land rights types. So, let’s take a closer look at the complicated factors that affect your options regarding Can Foreigners Purchase Real Estate in Bali and having property in this exotic paradise.

1. Right of Use (Hak Pakai)

The Hak Pakai, or Right of Use, is a good way for people who want to own a piece of Bali’s natural beauty. With this type of ownership, non-Indonesians can use and build on the land for up to 30 years, extending for another 20 years and renewing for up to 30 more years. Whether you’re pulled to the island’s quiet coast or its lush interior, the Hak Pakai allows you to make your safe place.

2. Right to Build (Hak Guna Bangunan)

Hak Guna Bangunan, known as the Right to Build, is for people who want to build what they want from the ground up. This type of ownership is based on a 30-year leasehold, which can be extended for another 20 years and renewed for up to 30 years. It allows you to build structures on the land that show your style and add to Bali’s architectural variety.

3. Freehold, or Hak Milik

People worldwide are interested in private ownership, but only Indonesian citizens can have it in Bali. But there is a way for outsiders to buy land with the help of an Indonesian nominee. Even though this is common, it’s important to be careful and ensure you work with reputable lawyers to protect your interests.

4. Company PMA

Setting up a foreign-owned company (PMA) is another way to own land in Bali for those who want to try something different. This method involves setting up a company that people from outside the country own. This company can buy land for specific uses, such as homes, businesses, or factories. This path requires following the law carefully and consulting with people who know Indonesian company law.
